AVTT Iniaitive

This is their story, how they see it.

A View Through Them (AVTT) stands to centralize the voices often underrepresented, misrepresented or, all together, overlooked in mainstream media, academia and classical canons. While this journey strives to honor and protect the diverse nature of humankind's record, it also acts as a reminder of the values and emotions we share as a people. The voices of the Indigenous, African, and most broadly people of color were, and still are, to a certain extent, historically cast aside. How can there be art without refrence to the situation of Earth's Black population? The AVTT Initiative is a contribution to the bigger picture: consciously filling in our master narrative, the collective memory, one voice at a time.

p.o.v.

point of view = “The question is not legitimacy or the ‘correctness’ of a point of view, but the difference between my point of view and theirs.”

- Toni Morrison, The Source of Self-Regard, 2019.

p.o.c.

person of color = Anyone that’s not white. To innerstand the views of Western philosophers, as well as our own philsophies, traditions of thought, and make best of them, I will first deconstruct and make sense of them for myself.

e.o.c.

educator of color = I challenge what education looks, feels and sounds like. As e.o.c. participate in this initiative they are also asserting their life narratives as enactments of resistance and self-definition. Discourse itself becomes agency.
