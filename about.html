A View Through Them

Center / About The Group

AVTT Initiative

This is their story, how they see it.

You’ve maybe heard this idea before: That so much of what we as children are taught is filtered through a predominately Eurocentric lens, usually not an Indigenous or an African one. The KeyAndre agrees, “some of the narratives that have been handed down through generations are filled with half truths and unquestioned assumptions…” Our proposed rhetorical question to you is: Can there be life on Earth without genuine reference to the situation of highly melanated people?

A View Through Them, stylized as AVTT, works to recognize the perspectives often underrepresented, misrepresented or, all together, deemed not available by conventional archives, academic libraries and classical canons. Expressions of the Indigenous, African or most broadly people of color were, and still are, to a certain extent, historically cast aside in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics as an educational category) and artistic related scholarship.

The KeyAndre commits itself to better involve narratives from the Indigenous or African diaspora, their experiences and the stories they want to hear from, learn about, and preserve… the stories that resonate with them. And while this initiative works diligently to honour and protect the diverse authenticity of humankind’s recorded story, it also acts as a reminder of the values, concerns, and emotions we humans share.

We at the KeyAndre hope this AVTT Initiative will contribute to a more collaborative, theoretical and inclusive dialogue around morality and ethics; the politics of difference and multicultural education; cultural competence amongst our children; the supremacy of mind and the vulnerability of body; the blessings and liabilities of power, accessibility in our future, class and disability.

KeyAndre and Company

About The Group's mission

KeyAndre & Company is a lifestyle brand that encompasses literature, media, and education, creating content with youth in mind. Established in 2016 by Kordell K. Hammond, the group is on a mission to encourage self-awareness, self-discipline and self-care through the art of design, rhetoric and rehabilitation.
