User Interface Voice Research (third-person)
Kordell K. Hammond is detail-oriented, which comes in handy when asked to think holistically about bias. He seeks to collaborate with tech engineers who are not necessarily positioned to see the social, political, or economic implications of the systems they build. For example, Speech Recongition, Audio Interpretation and Voice Search tools. Kordell does this to develop a more reflective and inclusive design process, for both teams and end-users.

A little more about his academic research: Influenced by a facination with ethical artificial intelligence (AI) in education settings, with a particular intrests in innovative, person-centric systems of teaching and leanring, Kordell also believes the ability to use natural language to communicate with our device(s) is a game-changer. He applies oral history methods and pattern-based techniques in his research, bringing the learnings from first-person oral history interviews to design, product, engineering, marketing and sales partners. This more thoroughly assists key user-centric decisions and practices.

Oral History Recording Component (third-person)
By audio recording the 1:1 sessions and preserving "animated," autobiographical data, interviewees can keep their voice-in-real-time and can experience the recorded session again; revisiting the tape for close-listening (aural) analysis and the transcription for close-reading (text) analysis. Kordell believes as businesses and, in return, education ecosystems scale in interfaces, algorithms, and infrastructure, it is imperative we further hone a critical ear toward empathy and compassion, improving UI/UX feedback through both the written and spoken word.
